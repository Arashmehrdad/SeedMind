## 1. Main question

> Does complete SeedMind provide measurable benefits over simpler versions?

Yes, within the declared deterministic symbolic Nursery scope, but not as a universal task-performance advantage.

Complete SeedMind showed measurable benefits in four bounded areas:

1. the reusable rollback production skill outperformed random primitive action on the familiar round-object suite;
2. ambition created persistent cross-episode capability commitment;
3. human apprenticeship converted justified help requests into resolved teaching events and support promotion;
4. replay, broad transfer testing, retention checks, and rollback prevented an unsupported specialist from being promoted.

Complete SeedMind did **not** outperform the frozen rollback controller on familiar task execution, because both intentionally used the same authoritative production action path. It also did not validate broad transfer for the rejected angular specialist.

Both conditions detected the repeated teacher outcome, but only the complete condition adopted and persisted an ambition. The result supports a narrow apprenticeship claim: the protected human channel adds measurable resolution and developmental progression beyond help detection alone.

## 11. What the numbers mean

The strongest Week 13 result is architectural rather than raw task dominance.

The complete system does not magically make the frozen controller better. Instead, it adds persistent developmental state, effective teaching resolution, and a governance process that blocks unsupported structural growth. Those benefits are measurable under matched action authority.

The 36 percent familiar-task result is meaningfully above the declared simple baselines, but it is not high absolute reliability. The 15/40 broader stress result reinforces that limitation. SeedMind has a reusable bounded skill, not a generally reliable object-manipulation policy.

The specialist result is a warning against narrow evaluation. Perfect performance on 52 designed cases did not transfer to any of 32 broader solvable cases. Week 13 supports the rollback process precisely because it refused to confuse narrow memorised competence with broad capability.

## 12. Honest failures and unexpected findings

1. **The fixed reactive baseline failed `0/100`.** The direct stateless heuristic was too weak for the scenario geometry. It provides a floor, not a competitive engineered baseline.
2. **The production controller succeeded only `36/100`.** This supports advantage over random action but exposes limited familiar-scenario coverage.
3. **The broader ball stress suite remained `15/40`.** Growth did not cause degradation, but the underlying general controller remains weak on the broader distribution.
4. **The specialist failed broad transfer `0/32`.** Its `52/52` narrow performance does not justify its broad `angular_push` label.
5. **Repeated deterministic seeds showed zero variance for ambition and teaching metrics.** This demonstrates consistency in the encoded symbolic cases, but the cases may be too clean to estimate robustness under noisy or ambiguous real interaction.
6. **Complete SeedMind has no task-success gain over rollback production.** This is by design because both use the same controller. Week 13 supports developmental-control benefits, not a fabricated controller advantage.
