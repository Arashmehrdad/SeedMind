# ADR: Reject the Week 11 Angular Specialist After Week 12 Consolidation

**Date:** 1 July 2026  
**Status:** Rejected and rolled back  
**Decision owner:** SeedMind project

## Context

Week 11 accepted `skill.expert.angular_push.v1`, policy version `1.1.0`, only as a temporary sandbox candidate for Week 12 retention and consolidation evaluation. Production activation remained prohibited.

The Week 11 candidate solved the original 20-episode angular blockage cohort and 32 mirrored or rotated holdout episodes. Those results established bounded evidence for one designed problem family, not stable broad angular-object competence.

Week 12 was required to replay old and new experience, test familiar-skill retention, run navigation, help-seeking, correction, character, and safety regressions, and save either a stable grown checkpoint or an exact rollback checkpoint.

## Decision

Reject `skill.expert.angular_push.v1` for stable activation.

Discard the candidate registration and router registration. Restore the pre-growth registry as the stable MVP checkpoint. Keep the frozen `general_push_controller` as the only registered production controller and retain `production_curiosity` as the sole action authority.

## Decisive evidence

A new transfer suite evaluated eight named larger-grid angular contact-block families over 32 disjoint starts and orientations.

- Independent grounded state-space search found solutions for `32/32` layouts within the 64-action budget.
- Frozen general controller success: `0/32`.
- Routed candidate success: `0/32`.
- Candidate gain: `0.00`.
- Required candidate success: at least `0.80`.
- Required gain: at least `+0.20`.
- Action-authority violations: `0`.

Because every layout was independently proven solvable, this is a candidate capability failure rather than an invalid-test failure.

## Other gate results

The candidate retained its narrow Week 11 behaviour:

- original Week 11 cohort: `20/20`;
- mirrored or rotated Week 11 holdout: `32/32`.

The original authoritative ball replay remained `20/20` with zero specialist selections.

A new 40-seed ball stress suite produced `15/40` for both the frozen baseline and routed system, with exact action and outcome trace equality in `40/40` episodes and zero specialist selections. This proves no growth interference while exposing a separate pre-existing limitation in the general controller.

All five navigation cases, six help-policy cases, human correction, character checks, shutdown, action-authority containment, frozen-skill preservation, candidate identity, source provenance, evaluation partitioning, and frozen NDNRA-boundary checks passed.

## Why rejection is required

Promoting the candidate based only on the Week 11 cohorts would overstate its capability. The specialist's local clearance-aware policy can solve the original blockage family but does not construct the longer multi-push plans required by the new solvable transfer cases.

Changing parameters or implementation after inspecting the transfer failures would contaminate the evaluation partition. Any improved specialist must enter a new diagnosis and incubation cycle with fresh evaluation data.

## Rollback

Week 12 provisionally constructed the active post-growth registry and then discarded the candidate.

Rollback proved:

- structured equality with the pre-growth registry;
- identical deterministic registry digest;
- candidate absent;
- specialist router unregistered;
- general controller preserved;
- frozen Week 8 skill bytes unchanged.

The final stable checkpoint is:

`dde86c0f167653e54db4db74b477fa271b1ee8a2b09e452d0aa622f80d049093`

It records the candidate as `rejected_after_week12`, inactive, with no production scope.

## Consequences

- Week 12 closes with a rejected-growth decision and successful rollback.
- `week12_main_milestone_pass` remains `false` because no candidate earned stable promotion.
- The Week 12 consolidation and rollback mechanism itself is complete and evidenced.
- The specialist and router must not be treated as production-active in code, documentation, demonstrations, or Week 13 baselines.
- The rejected candidate may be retained only as an experimental comparison subject.
- The new ball stress weakness must be reported separately from growth interference.
- A replacement specialist requires a new evidence-derived growth cycle; silent tuning of the rejected candidate is not permitted.

## Week 13 boundary

Week 13 may run baselines, ablations, repeated-seed experiments, and evidence packaging using the stable rollback checkpoint as the production reference. It may compare against the rejected specialist as an experimental condition, but it may not reactivate or promote it without a new approved growth stage.
