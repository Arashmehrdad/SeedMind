# ADR: Original SeedMind Plan and NDNRA Integration Decision

Date: 30 June 2026  
Status: Superseded on 1 July 2026 by `ADR-2026-07-01-freeze-ndnra-for-separate-project.md`
Scope: architecture direction only; runtime integration implementation is not approved

## Context

Standalone NDNRA v0.1 is closed under a bounded research scope. The original SeedMind master implementation plan remains a separate product and MVP roadmap covering the Nursery, predictive core, body discovery, curiosity, ambition, human apprenticeship, memory, reusable skill formation, contribution, capacity diagnosis, specialist growth, consolidation, retention, evaluation, observability, and competition delivery.

The two plans overlap in memory, recall, consequence learning, growth, consolidation, and retention, but they assign different responsibilities and use different evidence boundaries.

A comparison decision is required before any integration work can begin.

## Decision

The original SeedMind master implementation plan remains the governing product, MVP, safety, human-apprenticeship, evaluation, and competition roadmap.

NDNRA v0.1 is retained as a closed bounded research architecture and candidate cognitive substrate for selected future seams. It does not replace the complete SeedMind runtime.

The accepted high-level disposition is:

- retain the Nursery, perception encoder, recurrent predictive core, self-model, curiosity policy, ambition engine, apprenticeship manager, contribution flow, explicit skill contract, safety supervisor, production action controller, observatory goals, experiment programme, and competition roadmap;
- adapt the memory, checkpoint, metrics, testing, observatory, and roadmap interfaces around NDNRA;
- partially integrate only after separate approval where NDNRA supplies local delayed credit, need-driven recall, local consequence memory, recall effort, local saturation evidence, provenance, retention evidence, or bounded consolidation evidence;
- replace unrestricted SQLite action-memory lookup with NDNRA only after live Nursery evidence proves equivalent or better recall under the accepted safety and persistence gates;
- keep NDNRA proof stores, research growth, human-approved research operations, shadow suggestions, and bounded imagination isolated until a later ADR grants a narrower integration boundary;
- postpone semantic abstraction, learned context similarity, simultaneous multi-need coordination, unrestricted generic neuron creation, and a unified cognitive runtime checkpoint;
- reject proof-store merging, bounded-imagination persistence, implicit permission, autonomous workers or execution, automatic production growth, NDNRA production action authority, permanent deletion of NDNRA memory-bearing structures, and treating NDNRA action chains as automatic completion of the original Week 8 skill objective.

The original Week 8 objective remains required. NDNRA has not yet proved a reusable Nursery `approach_and_push` skill with explicit preconditions, termination, varied-start generalisation, reuse, known failure boundaries, and retention.

Production curiosity remains the sole production action authority. The protected safety supervisor and human permission channels remain external and authoritative.

Standalone acceptance persistence and long-horizon interference persistence remain separate isolated proof stores. Neither becomes a unified runtime checkpoint. Bounded imagination remains in memory and non-persistent.

The expanded developmental architecture marker remains 82% because this decision adds no new cognitive capability.

## Integration approval criteria

No runtime integration may be implemented until a later accepted ADR defines and proves:

1. one named responsibility and one authoritative owner;
2. matched-control behavioural invariance for all unaffected production paths;
3. measurable scientific or product advantage over the baseline;
4. explicit Week 8 skill equivalence before any skill replacement;
5. versioned atomic persistence, deterministic restart, complete fallback, and rollback;
6. retention within original task-degradation limits;
7. bounded resource and growth behaviour;
8. preserved human, safety, correction, stop, and permission authority;
9. no proof-store merging and no bounded-imagination persistence;
10. hardware, observability, reproducibility, and competition-claim fit.

## Consequences

- The comparison stage is complete and the selective-partial-integration direction is accepted as a recommendation.
- No integration implementation is authorized by this ADR.
- The original roadmap is not superseded by NDNRA closure.
- Original roadmap milestones may be re-baselined, but they may not be marked complete solely from NDNRA evidence.
- Week 8 reusable-skill work remains a distinct product requirement.
- SQLite remains valid for audit, beliefs, scientific evidence, and baseline comparison, but it is not approved as the future NDNRA cognitive recall path.
- Any later integration batch requires Arash's separate explicit approval before repository runtime changes begin.
