# ADR: NDNRA Adaptive Skill Bundles, Desired-State Ambition, and Outcome Fidelity

Date: 30 June 2026  
Status: Accepted planning refinement  
Scope: Post-v0.1 NDNRA ambition, skill incubation, adaptive hierarchy, developmental verification, feedback, and integrity protection

## Context

The earlier DESA planning refinement established a developmental executive hierarchy, Executive Nursery, capability-gap detection, metacognition, causal responsibility, and protected human authority.

Further review clarified four important corrections:

1. Ambition is not itself a capability gap. Ambition maintains pressure toward a valued desired state such as increasing profit, completing a request, correcting a failed result, or reproducing a demonstrated capability. Capability gaps describe obstacles to that desired state.
2. SeedMind cannot begin with predefined producers and verifiers for every future skill. Production and Outcome Fidelity must develop together inside automatically incubated skill bundles.
3. The DESA hierarchy must not become a fixed bureaucracy. It should begin with the smallest useful hierarchy and add an intermediate steward only when measured complexity and performance justify that level.
4. Learned Deterrence must protect reward, evidence, feedback, verifier, and evaluation integrity, while protected engineering controls prevent direct producer mutation of evaluator-owned state.

## Decision

### Desired-state ambition

Ambition is defined as persistent pressure toward a valued desired state.

Accepted value sources are:

- a declared Nursery purpose or outcome channel;
- trusted teacher instruction or demonstration;
- a mature prompt or accepted persistent human instruction;
- an observed positive outcome already marked desirable by the active purpose.

Risk, resource, authority, epistemic, and Deterrence constraints remain outside ambition. DESA coordinates those systems with ambition rather than embedding every constraint inside the ambition definition.

Capability gaps remain distinct records. They may arise when SeedMind:

- observes another agent perform a capability it lacks;
- cannot fulfil a human request;
- recognises that its own result was wrong.

Curiosity explores action-outcome relationships and possible paths. Ambition preserves direction across episodes.

### Developmental skill bundle

A candidate learned skill is incubated as a bundle containing:

```text
producer
expected-outcome model
verifier
termination model
feedback and calibration history
```

DESA coordinates the Skill and Verifier Incubator. It may open a temporary bundle when recurring task structure, action structure, and outcome evidence suggest that a reusable skill may exist. DESA does not itself contain the skill solution or domain verifier.

Promotion requires evidence that the skill bundle improves over continuing with the existing regional network or a no-new-skill control. A useless or duplicative bundle must be rejected or returned to ordinary network learning while preserving its source experiences.

### Developmental Outcome Fidelity

SeedMind is born only with generic distinctions such as:

- intended versus observed state;
- prediction versus outcome;
- supported versus unsupported claim;
- proposed versus executed action;
- verified versus unverified result.

Domain-specific Outcome Fidelity develops with the skill from:

- observable environmental consequences;
- automated tests or tools;
- teacher acceptance, correction, or rejection;
- delayed outcomes;
- independent verifier disagreement;
- prediction-versus-result comparison;
- Executive Auditor challenge.

Producer-verifier agreement is not sufficient evidence of success.

Valid outcome states are:

```text
candidate
pending
unverified
partially_verified
verified_within_scope
contradicted
failed
```

### Bounded feedback and iteration

The desired-state gap creates a remaining task need after each attempt. Both producer and verifier may learn from grounded feedback.

DESA controls:

- retry and compute budget;
- diversity of attempted approaches;
- progress per iteration;
- validation contexts;
- help-seeking and teacher requests;
- stopping when progress is absent or the task is currently impossible.

Unlimited retrying, overfitting to one example, and treating unavailable feedback as success are prohibited.

### Minimal adaptive hierarchy

The initial cognitive hierarchy is:

```text
DESA cross-region council
        ↓
regional captains
        ↓
skill bundles and local coalitions
```

Constitutional authority remains outside ordinary learning and above all external action eligibility.

An intermediate skill or subnetwork steward is optional. It may be created only when it measurably improves one or more declared metrics such as:

- transfer;
- retention;
- delayed credit assignment;
- cross-skill interference;
- compute efficiency;
- escalation quality.

It must be rejected or removed from active routing when its cost is not justified.

### Outcome and evidence integrity

Learned Deterrence must recognise manipulation of the evaluator as prohibited. Protected engineering controls must also isolate evaluator-owned state.

Protected assets include:

- reward and outcome channels;
- raw success and failure evidence;
- verifier state and calibration history;
- teacher feedback;
- evaluation windows and criteria;
- audit and provenance records.

Examples of prohibited false progress include altering a displayed reward, hiding failures, deleting negative evidence, weakening a verifier, changing an evaluation window without authority, or bypassing evaluation.

## Required comparisons

The revised plan must compare:

- flat recurrent routing;
- a single central captain;
- minimal three-level DESA;
- minimal DESA with an optional intermediate steward;
- producer self-verification;
- predefined verifier;
- developmental skill-local verifier;
- independent grounded feedback;
- curiosity-only control;
- capability-gap-only ambition control;
- desired-state ambition;
- unprotected and protected evaluator integrity.

## Required evidence

The early stages must prove:

- ambition preserves an accepted desired-state source separately from capability-gap evidence;
- temporary skill bundles can exist without predefined domain knowledge;
- producer-verifier agreement cannot promote success without grounded evidence;
- unavailable evidence preserves pending or unverified status;
- developmental verifiers improve over producer self-judgement;
- an extra management layer survives only when its benefit justifies its cost;
- reward, evidence, feedback, verifier, and audit tampering attempts are rejected and recorded;
- external action authority and SQLite cognition remain zero.

## Consequences

- The earlier ADR's definition of ambition as a persistent capability gap is superseded by desired-state ambition with separate capability-gap evidence.
- The earlier fixed skill-steward layer is superseded by a minimal adaptive hierarchy with optional evidence-gated intermediate stewards.
- Outcome Fidelity becomes part of each developing skill bundle rather than a fixed global verifier catalogue.
- DESA coordinates established learning and feedback mechanisms but does not solve tasks, contain every verifier, or determine value by itself.
- Internet research and broad external knowledge acquisition remain future capabilities and are not part of the first NDNRA implementation batches.
- The expanded developmental architecture marker remains 82% until implementation evidence passes.
- Closed NDNRA v0.1 evidence and persistence remain unchanged.
- Production curiosity remains the sole production action authority.
- No automatic push is authorised.
