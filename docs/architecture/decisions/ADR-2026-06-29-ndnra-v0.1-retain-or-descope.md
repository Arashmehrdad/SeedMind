# ADR: NDNRA v0.1 Retain-or-Descope Boundary

Date: 29 June 2026
Status: accepted
Scope: standalone NDNRA v0.1 completion

## Context

Standalone acceptance and restart proof now establish consolidated viability, exact reload, deterministic rerun equivalence, and safe non-proof fallback for the currently accepted NDNRA evidence. Seven expanded research questions remained unresolved or only partially represented.

NDNRA must close as a standalone research architecture before any comparison with or integration into the original SeedMind roadmap.

## Decision

The v0.1 closure boundary is:

### Retained as mandatory before closure

- local spreading-activation normalization under competing recruitment;
- locally derived representational saturation for growth pressure;
- long-horizon mixed-task interference and adaptability evidence.

### Already sufficiently represented for v0.1

- bounded initial connections for newly grown specialist interactions, using deterministic local eligibility, at most two positive-trace members per growth event, configured specialist limits, duplicate-membership blocking, and preservation of old assemblies.

### Deferred post-v0.1 research

- learned context-similarity weights;
- semantic abstraction above grounded context signatures;
- coordination of multiple simultaneous needs.

NDNRA v0.1 therefore explicitly supports one active need pulse at a time, grounded exact context signatures, fixed bounded contextual-transfer weights, and specialist-interaction growth rather than unrestricted generic neuron creation.

## Next stage

The next standalone stage is `Normalized Recruitment and Local Saturation`.

Normalization must be proven before saturation is derived, because a capacity signal over unnormalized recruitment would not establish stable behaviour as fan-in grows.

The stage must preserve deterministic heat-fan recall, deep-versus-shallow dormancy behaviour, local evidence, bounded computation, no SQLite cognition, and zero recommendation, execution, integration, or production action authority.

After that stage, `Long-Horizon Mixed-Task Interference and Adaptability` remains mandatory before final standalone closure.

## Consequences

- The 82% expanded architecture marker remains unchanged by this audit.
- Deferred capabilities cannot be described as implemented or solved in the final v0.1 closure.
- The final closure record must state the single-need, grounded-context, fixed-transfer, and specialist-growth scope explicitly.
- Existing bounded contextual transfer remains valid but does not become learned semantic similarity.
- Existing short interference evidence remains valid but does not satisfy the retained long-horizon requirement.
- Original-roadmap comparison and all integration decisions remain deferred until standalone NDNRA closure.
